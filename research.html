Theme 1 — Decoding Wnt/Frizzled Receptor Specificity

Theme 1 — Decoding Wnt/Frizzled Receptor Specificity
The Wnt pathway is a master regulator of stem cell fate, yet 19 Wnt ligands and 10 Frizzled (FZD) receptors generate a complex and poorly understood signaling code. We have developed synthetic tetravalent antibodies (“FLAgs”) that cluster selected FZD receptors with LRP5/6 co-receptors, mimicking Wnt ligands with engineerable specificity and stability
.
This approach allows selective activation of any individual FZD receptor in vitro and in vivo, overcoming the biochemical limitations of natural Wnt proteins.
By pairing receptor-specific agonists with cell-type expression maps, we can precisely control Wnt pathway activation in defined stem cell populations.

Theme 2 — Receptor-Specific Wnt Modulation for Regeneration and Therapy

Theme 2 — Receptor-Specific Wnt Modulation for Regeneration and Therapy
Our receptor-specific Wnt surrogate platform extends across vascular biology, tissue regeneration, and directed stem cell differentiation:
Vascular barrier repair: FZD4-selective agonists restore blood–retina barrier integrity, rescue congenital vascular defects, and normalize pathological neovascularization in retinopathy models
.
Organ regeneration: In the lung, FZD5- and FZD6-specific agonists promote alveolar type 2 epithelial stem cell activity, reprogram airway progenitors toward alveolar fate, and improve survival after injury while avoiding pro-fibrotic effects
. In the liver, single-cell and spatial transcriptomics revealed a zonal Wnt signaling circuit in which central vein endothelial cells secrete Wnt2 and Wnt9b to maintain hepatocyte identity and drive regeneration after injury. Importantly, we demonstrated that treatment with a receptor-specific Wnt agonist can restore Wnt activity in pericentral hepatocytes and promote liver regeneration, providing proof-of-concept for spatially targeted Wnt-based therapeutics.
Directed stem cell differentiation: FZD5 activation drives efficient generation of ventral midbrain dopaminergic neurons for Parkinson’s disease models
, while selective activation of FZD2 or FZD7 biases mesoderm differentiation toward paraxial or lateral fates
.
These studies demonstrate that receptor identity and tissue context dictate Wnt signal output and that this principle can be exploited to design cell-type- and region-specific regenerative therapies.

Theme 3 — Functional Genomics of Glioblastoma Stem Cells

Theme 3 — Functional Genomics of Glioblastoma Stem Cells
Glioblastoma (GBM) is an incurable brain cancer driven by glioblastoma stem cells (GSCs) that resist treatment and fuel recurrence. We have conducted the most extensive CRISPR-Cas9 functional genomics screens to date in patient-derived GSC cultures

.
Core genetic dependencies include transcription factors (SOX2, SOX9), chromatin regulators (DOT1L), cytokine signaling modulators (SOCS3), and stress-response pathways (ufmylation, ERAD).
By analyzing over 30 patient-derived lines, we uncovered state-specific vulnerabilities along a developmental ↔ injury-response continuum in GSCs, each with distinct signaling and cell cycle dependencies (e.g., CCND1 vs. CCND2).
Chemogenomic screens identified genes modulating temozolomide sensitivity and resistance, revealing opportunities for rational drug combinations.
These studies establish a functional blueprint of GBM stemness and heterogeneity, guiding precision therapeutic strategies for this highly variable cancer.
